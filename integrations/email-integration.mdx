## Why Connect Your Email?

Email attachments are one of the biggest sources of document chaos:

- Contracts buried in threads
- Invoices scattered across months of emails
- Reports you can never find when you need them
- Important documents lost in your inbox

With The Drive AI email integration:

- **Attachments are automatically imported** to your workspace
- **AI organizes them** into logical folders
- **Find anything instantly** with natural language search
- **Never lose an important document** again

## Connecting Gmail

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Integrations">
    Go to **Settings** > **Email Integrations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Gmail">
    Click **Connect Gmail**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize Access">
    Sign in to your Google account and grant The Drive AI permission to access your email attachments.
  </Step>
   <Step title="Import Past attachments">
    After successful authorization, you have an option to import past attachments:
    - You will see an option to import historical file attachments
    - Select how far back you want to go on history
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Importing">
    Click **Import** to start automatic attachment imports.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How It Works

### Automatic Import

Once connected, The Drive AI monitors your inbox for new attachments:

1. **New email arrives** with an attachment
2. **The Drive AI detects** the attachment
3. **Attachment is imported** to your workspace
4. **AI organizes** the file into the appropriate folder

All of this happens automatically—you don't need to do anything.

### What Gets Imported

By default, The Drive AI imports common document types:

- **Documents**: PDF, DOCX, DOC, TXT
- **Spreadsheets**: XLSX, XLS, CSV
- **Presentations**: PPTX, PPT
- **Images**: Images are not imported.

### Organization

Imported attachments are organized based on:

| Factor | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Content** | Invoice detected → Invoices folder |

## Configuration Options

### Blocking Email Addresses and Domains

You can block attachments from specific senders or entire domains to prevent unwanted files from being imported. Blocking can be configured at two levels:

**Integration Level**

Block senders for a specific email integration (Gmail or Outlook):

1. Go to **Settings** > **Email Integrations**
2. Select the integration you want to configure
3. Click **Blocked Senders**
4. Add email addresses (e.g., `spam@example.com`) or domains (e.g., `@marketing.com`)

**Workspace Level**

Block senders across all email integrations in your workspace:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Workspace** > **Email Blocklist**
2. Add email addresses or domains to the block list
3. These rules apply to all connected email accounts

<Tip>
  Use domain blocking (e.g., `@newsletters.com`) to block all emails from a specific domain at once.
</Tip>

### Import Frequency
The Drive AI imports files real-time.

## Fetching Past Email Attachments

You can import historical email attachments from both Gmail and Outlook. This allows you to bring in attachments from emails received before you connected The Drive AI.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Email Settings">
    Go to **Settings** > **Email Integrations** and select the connected account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Import History">
    Click **Import History** or **Fetch Past Attachments**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Date Range">
    Select the time period for historical import:
    - Last 30 days
    - Last 90 days
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start Import">
    Click **Start Import** to begin fetching historical attachments. The import runs in the background.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Historical import may take some time depending on the number of emails and attachments in the selected date range.
</Note>

## Activity History

Track all email attachment activity in your workspace with the Activity History feature. This gives you complete visibility into what files are being imported and their status.

### Viewing Activity History

1. Go to **Settings** > **Email Integrations**
2. Click **Activity History** or **View Activity**

### Activity Statuses

Each imported attachment shows one of the following statuses:

| Status | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Uploaded** | File was successfully imported to your workspace |
| **Skipped** | File was skipped based on your filter settings (blocked sender, excluded file type, etc.) |
| **Duplicate** | File was already imported previously and skipped to avoid duplicates |
| **Unsupported** | File type is not supported for import |

### Activity Details

For each activity entry, you can see:

- File name and type
- Sender email address
- Email subject
- Date and time of activity
- Status and any relevant notes

<Tip>
  Use the Activity History to troubleshoot import issues and understand why certain attachments may not have been imported.
</Tip>

### Email Metadata

Imported attachments retain metadata about their source:

- Sender email address
- Email subject
- Date received
- Original filename

## Managing Your Email Connection

### Disconnecting

To completely disconnect your email:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Integrations** > **Email**
2. Click **Disconnect** next to the email account
3. Confirm the disconnection

<Warning>
  Disconnecting does not delete already-imported attachments. It only stops future imports.
</Warning>

## Privacy & Security

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Attachments Not Importing" icon="file-circle-xmark">
    - Check that the email integration is enabled (not paused)
    - Verify the attachment file type is in your include list
    - Ensure you have sufficient storage space
    - Check that the email folder is being monitored
    - Check the **Activity History** to see if files were skipped or marked as unsupported
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Missing Old Attachments" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    By default, only new attachments (after connection) are imported. Use the **Fetch Past Attachments** feature to import historical attachments from before you connected. See [Fetching Past Email Attachments](#fetching-past-email-attachments) for details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Duplicate Files" icon="copy">
    Enable **Skip Duplicates** in your settings to prevent the same attachment from being imported multiple times. You can view duplicate detections in the **Activity History**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
